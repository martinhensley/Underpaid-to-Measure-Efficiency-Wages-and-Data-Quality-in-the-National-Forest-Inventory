# 6. Limitations

Several limitations of our analysis warrant explicit discussion.

**Geographic and temporal scope of paired data.** The paired QA/production analysis covers 24 northern states during 2011–2016. The absence of southern states means we cannot assess whether the HTCD differential varies by region, though the consistency across 24 states suggests a structural rather than regional pattern. Additionally, the HTCD field records the crew's self-reported method code; if crews systematically underreport estimation (recording HTCD=1 when they estimated), the 23.1% production rate is a lower bound on actual estimation prevalence.

**Selection versus incentives in paired comparison.** QA and production crews may differ on dimensions beyond monitoring incentives — experience, training intensity, supervisory oversight, workload pressure. The HTCD differential therefore reflects institutional differences broadly, not incentive effects in isolation. The policy prescription holds under multiple mechanisms, but the precise causal channel is not identified.

**Authorized estimation fraction unknown.** FIA protocols permit visual estimation in specific field conditions. We cannot determine from HTCD codes what fraction of production crew HTCD=3 records reflects authorized protocol use versus unauthorized shortcuts. The differential relative to QA crews provides a lower bound on excess estimation, but the absolute rate of unauthorized estimation is not identified.

**Binary effort assumption.** The model's binary effort choice overstates the discreteness of the actual decision. In practice, technicians choose from a continuum of effort levels. The continuous-effort extension (Holmström, 1979) yields qualitatively similar predictions but lacks the sharp threshold properties of the binary model. Calibration results indicate directional incentive pressures rather than precise wage thresholds.

**Effort cost parameterization.** The effort cost $\bar{e}$ is grounded in a time-budget micro-foundation ($\bar{e}/w \in [0.13, 0.36]$), but the wide range reflects substantial terrain and canopy variation. Of the calibrated parameters, the effort cost has the weakest direct empirical grounding: the time-budget approach provides bounds but not a point estimate. Specific NSC percentage gaps (e.g., the 78% and 43% benchmarks) should therefore be interpreted as indicative of direction and approximate magnitude rather than precise thresholds. Qualitative conclusions — that the NSC substantially exceeds current wages and that the detection probability is the dominant parameter — are robust across this range (Table 2).

**Crew composition and self-selection.** We lack crew-level identifiers in the public FIA data and cannot distinguish within-crew from between-crew composition effects. Career-track seasonals' tendency to stay late in the season means the seasonal signal predicted by the model would be attenuated even if terminal seasonals engage in estimation.

**Generalizability.** The analysis is specific to the U.S. FIA program's institutional design. Other national forest inventories may face similar or different incentive structures depending on their workforce model, monitoring intensity, and labor market context.
