# Annotated Bibliography

## Core Theoretical Framework

**Shapiro, Carl, and Joseph E. Stiglitz. 1984.** "Equilibrium Unemployment as a Worker Discipline Device." *American Economic Review* 74(3): 433–444.
→ Foundational efficiency wage paper. Core theoretical framework for the NSC derivation.

**Holmström, Bengt. 1979.** "Moral Hazard and Observability." *Bell Journal of Economics* 10(1): 74–91.
→ Informativeness principle; continuous-effort extension discussed in Section 2.5.

**Holmström, Bengt, and Paul Milgrom. 1991.** "Multitask Principal-Agent Analyses: Incentive Contracts, Asset Ownership, and Job Design." *Journal of Law, Economics, & Organization* 7(Special Issue): 24–52.
→ Multi-task moral hazard. Explains why effort reductions concentrate on hard-to-verify measurements (height > DBH).

**Akerlof, George A., and Janet L. Yellen, eds. 1986.** *Efficiency Wage Models of the Labor Market.* Cambridge University Press.
→ Edited volume establishing the broader efficiency wage literature.

## Intrinsic Motivation

**Bénabou, Roland, and Jean Tirole. 2003.** "Intrinsic and Extrinsic Motivation." *Review of Economic Studies* 70(3): 489–520.
→ External incentives can crowd out intrinsic motivation by signaling distrust. Section 2.5.

**Bénabou, Roland, and Jean Tirole. 2006.** "Incentives and Prosocial Behavior." *American Economic Review* 96(5): 1652–1678.
→ Monetary rewards/penalties can reduce overall effort by undermining prosocial motivation. Section 2.5.

**Frey, Bruno S., and Reto Jegen. 2001.** "Motivation Crowding Theory." *Journal of Economic Surveys* 15(5): 589–611.
→ Survey of crowding-out evidence from psychology and economics. Section 2.5.

**Perry, James L., and Lois Recascino Wise. 1990.** "The Motivational Bases of Public Service." *Public Administration Review* 50(3): 367–373.
→ Public service motivation concept. Section 2.5.

**Buurman, Margaretha, Josse Delfgaauw, Robert Dur, and Seth Van den Bossche. 2012.** "Public Sector Employees: Risk Averse and Altruistic?" *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization* 83(3): 279–291.
→ Public sector workers more altruistic than private sector. Section 2.5.

## Environmental/Resource Monitoring

**Duflo, Esther, Michael Greenstone, Rohini Pande, and Nicholas Ryan. 2013.** "Truth-telling by Third-party Auditors and the Response of Polluting Firms: Experimental Evidence from India." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 128(4): 1499–1545.
→ Closest existing work to our contribution. Auditor incentive design in environmental monitoring. Section 4.

**Olken, Benjamin A. 2007.** "Monitoring Corruption: Evidence from a Field Experiment in Indonesia." *Journal of Political Economy* 115(2): 200–249.
→ Increased audit probability reduces corruption. Parallels our q argument. Sections 2/5.

## Forest Economics and FIA

**Bechtold, William A., and Paul L. Patterson, eds. 2005.** *The Enhanced Forest Inventory and Analysis Program — National Sampling Design and Estimation Procedures.* USDA Forest Service GTR SRS-80.
→ Definitive FIA design reference. Section 2.

**Yanai, Ruth D., Alexander R. Young, John L. Campbell, James A. Westfall, Charles J. Barnett, Gretchen A. Dillon, Mark B. Green, and Christopher W. Woodall. 2023.** "Measurement Uncertainty in a National Forest Inventory: Results from the Northern Region of the USA." *Canadian Journal of Forest Research* 53(3): 163–177. doi:10.1139/cjfr-2022-0062.
→ Paired QA/production re-measurement analysis using 2,790 plots and 51,740 trees from the Northern Region (2010–2017). Sections 3, 6.

**Yanai, Ruth D., Alexander R. Young, John L. Campbell, James A. Westfall, Charles J. Barnett, Gretchen A. Dillon, Mark B. Green, and Christopher W. Woodall. 2023.** "Paired measurements from quality assurance visits to Forest Inventory and Analysis plots in the northern United States." Fort Collins, CO: Forest Service Research Data Archive. doi:10.2737/RDS-2022-0056.
→ Dataset: 94,459 paired tree observations across 24 northern states (2011–2016). Includes HTCD (height method code) enabling direct identification of ocular estimation. Section 3.1.

**Burns, Russell M., and Barbara H. Honkala, tech. coords. 1990.** *Silvics of North America.* Agriculture Handbook 654. Washington, DC: USDA Forest Service. 2 vols.
→ Comprehensive species-level growth data; basis for anomaly growth thresholds. Supplementary Materials A.5.

**Tomppo, Erkki, Thomas Gschwantner, Mark Lawrence, and Ronald E. McRoberts, eds. 2010.** *National Forest Inventories: Pathways for Common Reporting.* Springer.
→ International NFI comparison volume. Section 6.

**Fridman, Jonas, et al. 2014.** "Adapting National Forest Inventories to Changing Requirements — the Case of the Swedish National Forest Inventory." *Silva Fennica* 48(3): article 1095. [VERIFY exact title/pages]
→ Swedish NFI measurement quality and permanent workforce model. Section 6.

## Forensic Statistics

**Simonsohn, Uri. 2013.** "Just Post It: The Lesson from Two Cases of Fabricated Data Detected by Statistics Alone." *Psychological Science* 24(10): 1875–1888.
→ Detecting data fabrication via statistical forensics. Section 3 and Supplementary Materials.

**Mosimann, James E., Claire V. Wiseman, and Ruth E. Eddy. 2002.** "Data Fabrication: Can People Generate Random Digits?" *Accountability in Research* 9(1): 31–45. [VERIFY pages]
→ Digit pattern analysis for detecting fabrication. Supplementary Materials A.2.

**Nigrini, Mark J. 1996.** "A Taxpayer Compliance Application of Benford's Law." *Journal of the American Taxation Association* 18(1): 72–91.
→ Digit analysis for fraud detection. Supplementary Materials A.2.

## Novelty Positioning Note

No published work applies the Shapiro-Stiglitz efficiency wage framework specifically to natural resource data collection programs. Duflo et al. (2013) is the closest but addresses third-party auditing in a developing-country context. The novelty claim is well-supported.
